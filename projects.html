Residential net-load forecasting for renewable integration

Master's Thesis · GridLab, Lamar University · 2025–2026
PythonMATLABMachine LearningDeep LearningTime-Series Forecasting

Residential solar adoption is changing what utilities actually see on the meter: the "net load" curve, not the raw demand curve. This thesis builds forecasting models — spanning classical statistical methods, machine learning, and deep learning — to predict that net load using smart-meter readings and meteorological data.

  • Evaluated how distributed solar generation affects power system performance, to support renewable integration and grid reliability planning.
  • Designed feature engineering pipelines tailored to smart-meter and weather data.
  • Validated forecasting performance using both deterministic (point-forecast accuracy) and probabilistic (uncertainty-aware) evaluation metrics.

Spacecraft electric & magnetic field data analysis

Graduate Research Assistant · Dept. of Physics, Lamar University · 2025
PythonScientific Data ProcessingSignal Analysis

Working with the Physics department, I built Python-based computational tools to analyze electric and magnetic field measurements collected from spacecraft observations — applying signal-processing techniques to noisy, high-volume instrument data.

  • Processed and validated scientific datasets for downstream analysis.
  • Supported laboratory research, engineering analysis, and technical documentation for the wider research team.
