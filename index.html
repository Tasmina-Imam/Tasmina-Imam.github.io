About

Building tools that help the grid absorb more renewable energy

I'm an electrical engineer with a Master of Engineering from Lamar University, where I've spent the past two years researching how data science can improve power system reliability. My thesis work applies statistical, machine learning, and deep learning models to residential net-load forecasting using smart-meter and meteorological data — helping utilities anticipate demand as distributed solar generation grows.

Alongside that, I bring hands-on experience in electrical testing, instrumentation, and lab operations, plus a research background that spans power systems and space physics. I care about turning messy real-world data into models that engineers can actually rely on.
